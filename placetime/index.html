Base

Due to the duodecimal nature of the traditional notation a specific alphanumerical base of 4 × 15 = 60 characters is used to make human conversion easier.

ABCDEFGHIJKLMNO
PQRSTUVWXY01234
abcdefghijklmno
pqrstuvwxy56789

Now have a look at the conversion in detail.

Date

↓

The last digit represents a double-month (up to 62 days with the full alphanumerical base).

The next-to-last digit represents a decade (60 double months).

The remaining digits use a reduced base (A-Ya-y) to represent a "pentakose" (500 years = 50 decades), then 25.000 years, ...

The root of a 4-digit date is the year 20.000 BC, so that everything up to 5.000 AC is covered.

Time

↓

The three digits relate closely to traditional time-description. The first digit represents the hour, the second the minute, the third the second.

The first two digits span the same 11km²-raster on the globe as the traditional notation (360 degrees + one decimal).

  • One digit has an accuracy of 666km.
  • Two digits have an accuracy of 11km.
  • Three digits have an accuracy of 180m.
  • Four digits have an accuracy of 3m.
  • Five digits have an accuracy of 5cm.
